Emergency Bankruptcy Filing in Houston

When foreclosure, repossession, or wage garnishment is days away, an emergency bankruptcy filing can trigger the automatic stay immediately -- even before your full paperwork is complete.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Why Emergency Filings Matter in Texas

Texas has some of the fastest foreclosure timelines in the country. Non-judicial foreclosure can proceed in as few as 21 days from the first notice, and foreclosure sales happen on the first Tuesday of each month. If you are facing a scheduled foreclosure sale, an emergency filing may be the only way to stop it in time.

An emergency filing -- also called a bare-bones or skeleton petition -- files just enough paperwork to open your case and activate the automatic stay. You then have 14 days to complete and file the rest of your bankruptcy schedules.

What You Need to File

Filed Immediately

  • Voluntary Petition (Official Form 101)
  • Creditor matrix (list of all creditors with addresses)
  • Credit counseling certificate (must be completed before filing)
  • Filing fee ($338 for Ch. 7, $313 for Ch. 13) or fee waiver/installment application

Due Within 14 Days

  • Schedules A/B through J (assets, liabilities, income, expenses)
  • Statement of Financial Affairs
  • Chapter 7 means test (Form 122A) or Chapter 13 calculation (Form 122C)
  • Tax returns (last 4 years for the trustee)
  • Pay stubs (60 days prior to filing)
If you miss the 14-day deadline, your case will be automatically dismissed under 11 U.S.C. section 521(i), and all stay protection disappears.

Where to File in Houston

Emergency bankruptcy petitions in the Houston area are filed in the Southern District of Texas at the Houston Division courthouse located at 515 Rusk Ave, Houston, TX 77002. Cases can also be filed electronically through an attorney's PACER/CM-ECF account, which is often faster.

The S.D. Tex. is one of the busiest bankruptcy courts in the country. Emergency filings are processed quickly, and the automatic stay takes effect the moment the case number is assigned -- typically within minutes of electronic filing.

Common Emergency Scenarios

Foreclosure Sale Scheduled

Texas foreclosure sales occur the first Tuesday of each month. If you receive a Notice of Acceleration or Notice of Sale, an emergency filing before the sale date will stop the sale. Texas has an unlimited homestead exemption (up to 10 acres urban), making Chapter 13 especially powerful for saving your home.

Vehicle Repossession

If a repo agent is actively seeking your vehicle, filing triggers the stay immediately. Texas exempts one vehicle per licensed household member if needed for work. Once the stay is in place, the creditor must return a repossessed vehicle or face sanctions.

Wage Garnishment

Texas does not allow wage garnishment for most consumer debts -- one of only four states with this protection. However, garnishment is allowed for child support, taxes, and student loans. An emergency filing stops even these garnishments.

Bank Account Levy

If a creditor has obtained a judgment and is about to levy your bank account, an emergency filing stops the levy. Any funds already seized may need to be returned depending on applicable exemptions.

Repeat Filers: Reduced Stay Protection

If you had a bankruptcy case dismissed within the past year, your automatic stay may be limited:

  • One prior dismissal: The stay lasts only 30 days unless extended by court order (section 362(c)(3))
  • Two or more prior dismissals: No stay at all unless the court orders it (section 362(c)(4))

This is critical for emergency filers. If you have a prior dismissal, you may need to file a motion to extend or impose the stay at the same time as your emergency petition. Check your eligibility with the 1328(f) discharge screener.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an emergency bankruptcy filing?

An emergency bankruptcy filing is when you file just the petition and essential documents to activate the automatic stay immediately. You then have 14 days to file remaining schedules and statements.

When should I consider an emergency filing in Houston?

Consider it when facing an imminent foreclosure sale (Texas allows non-judicial foreclosure in as few as 21 days), scheduled repossession, active garnishment for non-exempt debts, a pending bank levy, or utility shutoff. File at the S.D. Tex. courthouse at 515 Rusk Ave.

What documents do I need for an emergency filing?

At minimum: the voluntary petition (Form 101), creditor matrix, credit counseling certificate, and the filing fee or fee waiver. All remaining schedules must be filed within 14 days.

What happens if I miss the 14-day deadline?

Your case is automatically dismissed under 11 U.S.C. section 521(i). The stay disappears and creditors can resume collection. You may request a brief extension for cause.

Does the automatic stay work immediately in an emergency filing?

Yes. The stay under section 362 takes effect the instant the petition is filed. However, if you had a prior dismissal within the past year, the stay may be limited or absent.

How much does an emergency filing cost in Houston?

The filing fee is $338 for Chapter 7 and $313 for Chapter 13. Attorney fees may be higher for emergency work. Fee waivers are available for Chapter 7 if your income is below 150% of the poverty line.
