Houston Federal Bankruptcy Court Guide

Everything you need to know about the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Texas. Address, filing hours, local rules, trustee panel, and pro se resources.

Court Location and Contact

United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Texas

Bob Casey U.S. Courthouse, 515 Rusk Ave, Suite 6000, Houston, TX 77002
Phone: (713) 250-5500
Website: https://www.txsb.uscourts.gov
CM/ECF: https://ecf.txsb.uscourts.gov
PACER: pacer.uscourts.gov

Filing Hours and Procedures

Filing Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM CT. Electronic filing (CM/ECF) available 24/7.

Filing Fees: Chapter 7: $338 | Chapter 13: $313 | Chapter 11: $1,738

Fee Waivers: Available for Chapter 7 filers with income below 150% of the federal poverty line. Chapter 13 filers may request installment payments.

Electronic Filing: Attorneys must file through CM/ECF. Pro se debtors may file on paper at the clerk's window or request CM/ECF access.

Local Rules: https://www.txsb.uscourts.gov/local-rules

Trustee Panel Information

Chapter 7 trustees are assigned from the U.S. Trustee panel for Region 7. Standing Chapter 13 Trustees serve the Houston Division.

The U.S. Trustee Program oversees the administration of bankruptcy cases and maintains the panel of private trustees. You can find the current trustee panel at justice.gov/ust.

341 Meeting of Creditors

Location: 341 meetings for the Houston Division are held at the Bob Casey U.S. Courthouse, 515 Rusk Ave, Houston, TX 77002, or at designated trustee meeting facilities, or by phone/video.

What to Bring: Government-issued photo ID, proof of Social Security number, most recent tax return, recent pay stubs, and recent bank statements.

Timing: The 341 meeting is typically scheduled 20-40 days after filing. You will receive a notice with the exact date, time, and format. For more: 341meeting.org.

Pro Se Resources

The S.D. Texas provides pro se resources through the clerk's office. Lone Star Legal Aid and Houston Volunteer Lawyers may provide free assistance.

Required Forms: All bankruptcy forms are available at uscourts.gov/forms/bankruptcy-forms.

Credit Counseling: You must complete credit counseling from an approved agency before filing. List of approved agencies: justice.gov/ust.

For more pro se filing resources: prosebankruptcy.org | filebankruptcywithoutlawyer.com.

Exemptions: Texas has extremely generous exemptions. The homestead exemption protects your primary residence with no cap on value (up to 10 acres urban, 100/200 acres rural). Texas uses state exemptions under Tex. Prop. Code sec. 41 and sec. 42.

For detailed exemption information: bankruptcyexemptionsbystate.com.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file bankruptcy in Houston?

File at the Bob Casey U.S. Courthouse, 515 Rusk Ave, Houston, TX 77002. Complete credit counseling first, prepare your petition and schedules, and pay the filing fee. Attorneys must file electronically through CM/ECF.

What are the filing fees in the Southern District of Texas?

Chapter 7 costs $338 and Chapter 13 costs $313. You can pay in installments or apply for a fee waiver. Attorney fees in Houston typically range from $1,000-$2,500 for Chapter 7 and $2,500-$5,000 for Chapter 13.

Why is the Chapter 13 rate so high in Houston?

The S.D. Texas has a Chapter 13 filing rate above 60%, well above the national average. This is driven by local attorney practices favoring Chapter 13 for homeowners, strong trustee administration, and Texas exemption laws.

Is the S.D. Texas the same as the court for big corporate bankruptcies?

Yes. The S.D. Texas, particularly the Houston Division, has become one of the top venues for large Chapter 11 corporate bankruptcies. However, the vast majority of cases filed are consumer Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 cases.

Where are 341 meetings held in Houston?

341 meetings for the Houston Division are held at the Bob Casey U.S. Courthouse, 515 Rusk Ave, or at designated trustee meeting facilities, or by phone/video. Your notice specifies the exact format, date, and time.
