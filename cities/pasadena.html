Bankruptcy in Pasadena, TX

Pasadena is the second-largest city in Harris County, located southeast of downtown Houston near the Houston Ship Channel. With a large working-class population and significant petrochemical industry presence, Pasadena residents face financial pressures from industry downturns, medical debt, and cost-of-living increases.

Texas Exemptions for Pasadena Residents

Pasadena is entirely within Harris County. Texas exemptions protect your home (unlimited value, up to 10 urban acres), one vehicle per licensed household member, and up to $50,000 in personal property ($100,000 for families). Texas also exempts 75% of wages from garnishment (90% for head of household), and retirement accounts are fully protected.

Exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. See the full Texas exemptions guide.

What to Expect Filing from Pasadena

The bankruptcy courthouse at 515 Rusk Ave in downtown Houston is approximately 15 miles northwest of Pasadena. After filing, the automatic stay immediately stops wage garnishments, collection calls, lawsuits, and repossession attempts. Your 341 meeting will be scheduled about 30 days after filing.

The Filing Process

  1. Credit counseling - Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25). Available online 24/7.
  2. File your petition - Your petition is filed with the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division. The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and foreclosures.
  3. 341 meeting - About 30 days after filing, attend a brief meeting of creditors. Bring photo ID and your Social Security card. Many meetings are now by phone.
  4. Debtor education - Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge - Chapter 7: approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year repayment plan.

Local Resources for Pasadena Residents

Pasadena residents have access to Lone Star Legal Aid (713-652-0077), Houston Volunteer Lawyers (713-228-0732), and the South Texas College of Law legal clinics. Many legal aid organizations provide bilingual services for Pasadena's significant Spanish-speaking population.

Before You File

  • Gather your financial documents: pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements, and a list of all debts
  • Complete a pre-filing credit counseling course from a U.S. Trustee-approved provider
  • Consult with a qualified bankruptcy attorney about your specific situation
  • Understand the difference between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13
  • Check whether a prior discharge affects your eligibility using the 1328(f) screener

Frequently Asked Questions - Pasadena, TX

Are there Spanish-speaking bankruptcy resources in Pasadena?

Yes. Lone Star Legal Aid and Houston Volunteer Lawyers both offer bilingual services. Many bankruptcy attorneys in the Houston area speak Spanish. Court-approved credit counseling courses are available in Spanish. The bankruptcy court also provides interpreters when needed.

Can I file bankruptcy if I was laid off from the petrochemical industry?

Yes. Unemployment or reduced income can actually make it easier to qualify for Chapter 7 because the means test looks at your average income over the six months before filing. A recent job loss may help you pass the means test.

Will bankruptcy stop a car repossession in Pasadena?

Yes. Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately stops repossession. In Chapter 13, you can catch up on missed car payments. In Chapter 7, you can reaffirm the debt to keep the vehicle. Texas allows you to exempt at least one vehicle per licensed household member.
