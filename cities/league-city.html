Bankruptcy in League City, TX

League City is the largest city in Galveston County, located between Houston and Galveston along the I-45 corridor near NASA's Johnson Space Center. Despite its growth and prosperity, residents can face financial challenges from medical bills, credit card debt, or unexpected expenses.

Texas Exemptions for League City Residents

League City straddles Galveston and Harris Counties. All residents file in the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division (some Galveston County cases may be assigned to the Galveston Division). Texas exemptions fully protect your homestead. Personal property exemptions cover up to $50,000 per individual ($100,000 for families). Higher incomes near the aerospace industry may require Chapter 13 rather than Chapter 7.

Exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. See the full Texas exemptions guide.

What to Expect Filing from League City

The Houston bankruptcy courthouse at 515 Rusk Ave is about 30 miles northwest. Some Galveston County cases are heard at the Galveston federal courthouse (601 25th St). After filing, expect the 341 meeting within 30 days. Many are conducted telephonically. The automatic stay protects you from all collection activity from the moment you file.

The Filing Process

  1. Credit counseling - Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25). Available online 24/7.
  2. File your petition - Your petition is filed with the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division. The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and foreclosures.
  3. 341 meeting - About 30 days after filing, attend a brief meeting of creditors. Bring photo ID and your Social Security card. Many meetings are now by phone.
  4. Debtor education - Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge - Chapter 7: approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year repayment plan.

Local Resources for League City Residents

Lone Star Legal Aid (713-652-0077) serves Galveston County. The Galveston County Bar Association offers referrals. University of Houston Law Center and South Texas College of Law clinics may accept Galveston County residents.

Before You File

  • Gather your financial documents: pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements, and a list of all debts
  • Complete a pre-filing credit counseling course from a U.S. Trustee-approved provider
  • Consult with a qualified bankruptcy attorney about your specific situation
  • Understand the difference between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13

Frequently Asked Questions - League City, TX

Will my case be heard in Houston or Galveston?

League City cases are typically filed in the Houston Division (515 Rusk Ave, Houston). However, some Galveston County cases may be assigned to the Galveston Division (601 25th St, Galveston, TX 77550). Your filing attorney can advise which division applies.

Can I protect my boat if I file bankruptcy?

A boat may be protected under Texas's personal property exemptions ($50,000 individual / $100,000 family). If used for your livelihood, it may qualify for tools-of-trade protection. Recreational boats of high value may be at risk in Chapter 7.

How does hurricane damage affect my bankruptcy options?

If you have disaster-related debts or insurance shortfalls, bankruptcy can discharge those unsecured obligations. FEMA disaster assistance is not considered income for the means test. Your homestead exemption still applies to hurricane-damaged property.
