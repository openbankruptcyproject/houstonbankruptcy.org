Bankruptcy in Friendswood, TX

Friendswood is a suburban city straddling Galveston and Harris Counties between Houston and the Gulf Coast. Known for excellent schools and family orientation, Friendswood has a higher median income than much of the Houston metro. Nevertheless, medical crises, job loss, or natural disasters can overwhelm even well-established households.

Texas Exemptions for Friendswood Residents

Friendswood spans two counties but all residents file in the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division. Texas's unlimited homestead exemption is particularly valuable in Friendswood where home values are above average. Personal property exemptions protect up to $50,000 per individual ($100,000 for families). Friendswood was significantly impacted by Hurricane Harvey.

Exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. See the full Texas exemptions guide.

What to Expect Filing from Friendswood

The Houston bankruptcy courthouse at 515 Rusk Ave is approximately 25 miles northwest. After filing, the automatic stay immediately stops all creditor activity. Your 341 meeting is scheduled about 30 days after filing and may be by phone. Chapter 7 typically concludes in 3-4 months.

The Filing Process

  1. Credit counseling -- Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25). Available online 24/7.
  2. File your petition -- Your petition is filed with the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division. The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and foreclosures.
  3. 341 meeting -- About 30 days after filing, attend a brief meeting of creditors. Bring photo ID and your Social Security card. Many meetings are now by phone.
  4. Debtor education -- Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge -- Chapter 7: approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year repayment plan.

Local Resources for Friendswood Residents

Friendswood residents can access Lone Star Legal Aid (713-652-0077), the Galveston County Bar Association, and the Harris County Law Library. Financial counseling through nonprofit agencies is available. Court-approved credit counseling is online.

Before You File

  • Gather your financial documents: pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements, and a list of all debts
  • Complete a pre-filing credit counseling course from a U.S. Trustee-approved provider
  • Consult with a qualified bankruptcy attorney about your specific situation
  • Understand the difference between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13
  • Check whether a prior discharge affects your eligibility using the 1328(f) screener

Frequently Asked Questions -- Friendswood, TX

Is Friendswood in the Houston or Galveston Division?

Friendswood cases are typically filed in the Houston Division (515 Rusk Ave, Houston, TX 77002). Some Galveston County cases may be assigned to the Galveston Division.

Can I protect my home equity in Friendswood?

Yes. Texas's homestead exemption is unlimited in value for your primary residence on up to 10 urban acres. Friendswood homes are fully protected regardless of how much equity you have.

What is the means test income limit?

The means test uses Texas statewide median income figures, updated periodically. For a household of four, the median is approximately $100,000 (check the U.S. Trustee's website for current figures). If below the median, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7.
